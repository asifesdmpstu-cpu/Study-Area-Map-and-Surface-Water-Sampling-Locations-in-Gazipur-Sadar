# Study Area Map and Surface Water Sampling Locations in Gazipur Sadar

##  Project Overview
This repository features a comprehensive Study Area Map of Gazipur Sadar, Bangladesh, focusing specifically on the Mirzapur and Kayaltia regions. The map was developed using Geographic Information System (GIS) tools to precisely highlight 20 surface water sampling points along with the Bonshai River and adjacent waterbodies. This spatial visualization is a critical component for environmental research, specifically in assessing heavy metal contamination and water quality in surface waters adjacent to low-density industrial sites.

##  Map Features & Details
The cartographic layout is designed for academic and research publications, providing a clear transition from a national scale down to specific sampling locations. Key features include:

*   **National & Regional Context:** Inset maps showing the location of Gazipur Sadar within Bangladesh.
*   **Sampling Points:** 20 distinct, precisely georeferenced locations (marked with red pins) for surface water collection.
*   **Hydrological Features:** Clear delineation of the Bonshai River and surrounding local waterbodies.
*   **Standard Cartographic Elements:** Includes a North arrow, scale bar, grid coordinates, and a comprehensive legend.

##  Technologies & Tools Used
*   **GIS Software:** ArcGIS (for spatial data visualization, georeferencing, and layout composition)
*   **Data Types:** Vector data (Shapefiles for administrative boundaries, rivers, and point coordinates)
*   **Domain:** Environmental Science, Water Quality Monitoring, Spatial Analysis

##  Key Objectives
*   To visually establish the geographic context of the environmental research study.
*   To map the exact coordinates of 20 surface water sampling sites for accurate spatial representation.
*   To provide a high-quality, professional base map to support findings related to environmental chemistry and pollution indexing.
